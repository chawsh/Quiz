Question 5

According to the National Prospective Post-Tonsillectomy Audit (2005), risk factors for postoperative bleeding include:

  1. A) Children
  2. B) Adults
  3. C) Tonsillectomy performed for quinsy
  4. D) Use of diathermy
  5. E) Use of blunt dissection

The National Prospective Post-Tonsillectomy Bleeding Audit reported:

  • Incidence of primary haemorrhage was 0.6%; the majority in the first 6 hours
  • Adults were at a higher risk than children
  • Operations for quinsy (retropharyngeal abscess) and recurrent tonsillitis had the highest rates
  • Blunt dissection techniques had lower rates than diathermy

Question 6

During laser surgery for removal of a vocal cord polyp, a supraglottic airway fire occurs in the patient; the following therapeutic measures are appropriate:

  1. A) Immediate discontinuation of the laser
  2. B) Flooding of the supraglottic area with hypertonic saline
  3. C) Attending to the patient with an ABC approach and ventilating with 100% oxygen
  4. D) Maintenance of a definitive airway with the existing endotracheal tube
  5. E) High-dose intravenous steroids

Immediate management of airway fire:

  • Stop the laser and flood the area with normal saline or sterile water.
  • Disconnect the ETT from the breathing system.
  • Immediately clamping the end of ETT.
  • Witholding jet ventilation if in use.
  • Reinstituting ventilation when the fire is extinguished.
  • Remove debris with suction
  • Replace the ETT with exchange catheter. Burnt ETT may risk blowing debris into the airway.
