  1. Question

    The waiting time (in minutes) at the cashier of two supermarket chains with different cashier systems is compared. The following statistical test was performed:

    
    Two Sample t-test

data:  Waiting by Supermarket
t = -0.25973, df = 126, p-value =
0.3977
alternative hypothesis: true difference in means between group Sparag and group Consumo is less than 0
95 percent confidence interval:
      -Inf 0.8756008
sample estimates:
 mean in group Sparag 
             5.240643 
mean in group Consumo 
             5.403401

    Which of the following statements are correct? (Significance level 5%)


    1. The absolute value of the test statistic is larger than 1.96.
    2. A one-sided alternative was tested.
    3. The p-value is larger than 0.05.
    4. The test shows that the waiting time is longer at Sparag than at Consumo.
    5. The test shows that the waiting time is shorter at Sparag than at Consumo.

    Solution

    1. False. The absolute value of the test statistic is equal to 0.26.
    2. True. The test aims at showing that the difference of means is smaller than 0.
    3. True. The p-value is equal to 0.398.
    4. False. The test aims at showing that the alternative that the waiting time is shorter at Sparag than at Consumo. The test result is not significant (p≥0.05p \ge 0.05).
    5. False. The test result ist not significant (p≥0.05p \ge 0.05).

    Adrenaline


    1. Is metabolised in the liver
    2. Is used in an intravenous dose of 50 micrograms (0.5 mL of 1:100000 solution) in the treatment of anaphylaxis
    3. Is used in an intravenous dose of 1 mg in the treatment of asystole in adults
    4. Stimulates gylcogenolysis
    5. Is sysnthesised from noradrenaline by a methyl transfererase in the adrenal medulla and nerve endings

    Solution

    1. True. It is metabolised in the liver.
    2. False. 50 micrograms intravenous injection (0.5 mL of 1:10 000 concentration solution or 5 mL of 1:100 000 solution)
    3. True. In the treatment of adult cardiac arrest, a bolus dose of 1 mg intravenous (10 mL of 1:10 000 concentration solution) is used.
    4. True. Adrenaline increased glycogenolysis.
    5. True. Tyrosine-DOPA-Dopamine-Noradrenaline-Adrenaline. Noradrenaline is converted in the adrenal medulla and some neurones to adrenaline (catalysed by PNMT.
